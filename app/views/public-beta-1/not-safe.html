Getting help if you have experienced abuse

This service may not be right for you if you've experienced domestic abuse.

If you feel confident that you can carry on without any danger to yourself or the children, you can continue using the service.

Get help protecting yourself and your children

You may have been in an abusive relationship. Domestic abuse or violence is a crime. You can find out how to recognise domestic abuse.

If you have experienced any kind of domestic abuse, you can get a court order to protect you or your child.

Help and support

There is free, confidential support available to help you and your family.

Refuge National Domestic Abuse Helpline

Free, confidential advice, 24 hours a day if you need help understanding your options, finding a refuge space or other specialist services.

Telephone: 0808 2000 247
Rights of Women

Family law advice from a charity dedicated to providing free and confidential legal advice to women experiencing all forms of violence in England and Wales.

Telephone: 020 7251 6577
Men's Advice Line

A supportive helpline for all male victims of domestic abuse and those supporting them.

Telephone: 0808 801 0327
